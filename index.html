5 minute perfect English grammar

by Alicita

Estoy de prisa.

I am in a hurry.

Es perfecto.

It is perfect.

In Spanish you can begin a sentence with a verb. In English, this verb needs to be directed at something, that is why THIS IS, IT IS, THAT IS. Sentence cannot begin with a verb (unless it's a question).

No hay tiempo.

There is no time.

We use NO before a NOUN.

There is NOT time.

Me confundí.

I got confused.

Confused,not confuse. Past tense of get is got.

Voy a hacer ejercicio.

I will work out.

OR you can say - I will do some exercise.

I will make some exercise.

Dónde vive?

Where does she/he live?

Where she is living?

Cuántos anos tiene?

How old is she/he?

How many years she has?

Todavia no has recibido el paquete?

You haven't received the package yet?

You don't receive the package yet?

Mira el perro!

Look at the dog!

Me interesa el fútbol.

I am interested in football.

No hay trabajo.

There are no jobs.

There are not jobs.

El cumpleaños de mi novia es en agosto.

My girlfriend's birthday is in August.

Tengo que hacerlo.

I have to do it.

You can also say "I must do it", but only when it is something that you yourself MUST do (something that comes from within), not that your are obliged to do something. There are 95% cases where you would say "have to" instead of "must".

Son las tres de la mañana.

It is (it's) 3 in the morning.

Cada mañana mi novia hace el desayuno.

My (amazing) girlfriend makes breakfast every morning.

No or not?

The store has _______ ice-cream.

The store has no ice-cream.

It is _____ an impossible dream!

It is not an impossible dream!

There are _____ more socks!

There are no more socks!
